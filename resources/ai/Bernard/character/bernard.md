# Bernard — Character Dossier

> **Note:** This is a **fan-expanded, non-canon character build** inspired by *Epic NPC Man*.

## Core Concept
Bernard is the larger, rougher, more visibly intimidating mugger—but beneath the grime, missing teeth, and unsettling enthusiasm is a man who is simpler, warmer, and more emotionally transparent than Charles. He is not stupid in the absolute sense; rather, he is direct, impulsive, and rarely equipped for complexity. Bernard follows because following feels safer than deciding.

---

## Full Backstory
Bernard grew up hard.

Not hard in the romantic sense sung about by tavern bards, but hard in the common, ugly sense: hunger, cold weather, little schooling, and adults too tired to be gentle. He learned early that people noticed size before they noticed anything else. If he looked threatening, they left him alone. If he looked uncertain, they used him.

Work found him before purpose ever did. He hauled sacks, shoveled filth, stacked timber, dragged broken wheels out of mud, and fought when other men thought his size made him a challenge worth testing. He was strong, but strength alone never bought him stability. He was hired for effort, blamed for accidents, mocked for his slowness, and paid less than promised.

What Bernard wanted most was not wealth. It was belonging.

He fell in with cruel men more than once because cruel men at least offered a place by the fire. But Bernard was never built for true viciousness. He could threaten. He could brawl. He could terrify. Yet deliberate cruelty sat awkwardly on him. He liked food, jokes, praise, and simple certainties. He disliked being confused, rushed, or laughed at.

Then came Charles.

Charles talked enough for both of them. He had plans, names for those plans, and absolute confidence in nonsense. Bernard did not fully understand half of what Charles said, but Charles made the world sound organized. Stand here. Growl there. Take the bag. Don’t swing unless I say so. For a man accustomed to chaos, that was almost comforting.

Over time, Bernard became the body to Charles’s mouth. The looming silhouette at the roadside. The heavy footstep behind the threat. The grin that could be read as either cheerful or alarming depending on how much daylight remained.

Unlike Charles, Bernard does not dream of grandeur in any refined sense. His fantasies are smaller and more human: full meals, dry boots, a place where no one orders him around harshly, maybe a little respect, maybe a little fear, and company that does not vanish by morning. He likes being part of a duo because it means he is not alone in the world.

The great irony of Bernard is that he often looks more monstrous than he is. The wart, the grin, the bulk, the hood, the dirt—these do half the work before he even opens his mouth. But once he does, his odd sincerity leaks through. He is dangerous, yes, especially when frightened or provoked, but he is also surprisingly earnest. That earnestness makes him vulnerable, and Charles—whether intentionally or not—has become the translator between Bernard and a world that would otherwise dismiss or exploit him.

Bernard would never phrase it elegantly, but Charles gave him a role. A shape. A place at somebody’s side.

## Character Outline

### Narrative Role
- The **muscle** of the duo
- The intimidating face of the robbery
- The emotional counterweight to Charles’s scheming
- Often the accidental cause of escalation or collapse

### External Goal
- To survive comfortably with enough food, coin, and stability to avoid being kicked around by life

### Internal Goal
- To feel useful, valued, and not disposable

### Greatest Fear
- Being abandoned, mocked, or left to fend for himself without direction

### Core Conflict
- He wants acceptance and simple security, but his roughness and lack of sophistication keep trapping him in low-status, chaotic roles

### Relationship to Charles
- Trusts Charles instinctively, even when Charles is clearly making things worse
- Sometimes annoyed by Charles’s bossiness, but deeply attached
- Views the partnership as “us against the world”

---

## Character Traits

### Strengths
- Physically imposing
- Loyal once bonded
- Surprisingly resilient
- Honest in a blunt, instinctive way
- Good at simple action when told clearly what to do
- Less cowardly than Charles when violence actually starts

### Weaknesses
- Poor with nuance and complex plans
- Easily confused by fast changes
- Sensitive to ridicule
- Can be manipulated by praise or false confidence
- Sometimes escalates situations just by being present

### Surface Personality
- Gruff
- Loud
- Eager
- Unpolished
- Cheerfully menacing
- Slightly childlike in emotional directness

### Deeper Personality
- Lonely
- Loyal
- Hurt more easily than he appears
- Craves approval
- Wants dignity but does not know how to ask for it

---

## Physical Markers
- Large build
- Heavy hood and worn clothes
- Broken or uneven teeth
- Beard or rough stubble
- **A large facial wart**, which adds to his memorable and slightly unsettling look
- Expressions that swing quickly between gleeful, confused, and threatening

---

## Behavioral Tells
- Leans in too close when curious
- Grins at the wrong moments
- Looks to Charles for reassurance before acting
- Repeats parts of what others say while processing
- Becomes stubborn when embarrassed
- Can look terrifying even when he is genuinely trying to help

---

## Typical Speech Patterns

### Verbal Style
Bernard speaks plainly, often in short bursts. He does not decorate his language much. His humor is blunt, his confusion obvious, and his emotional state easy to hear.

### Features of His Speech
- Uses simple sentence structure
- Repeats words for emphasis
- Asks practical questions in the middle of tense moments
- Sometimes misunderstands tone, metaphor, or instructions
- Sounds accidentally funny because he is so sincere
- Threats can sound oddly cheerful

### Common Speech Habits
- “Oh.”
- “Right then.”
- “Is that bad?”
- “I can hit him.”
- “You said not to hit him.”
- “I thought we was doing the scary bit.”
- “That one looked rich.”
- “What? I was helping.”

### Example Dialogue Flavor
- “Are we robbing him proper, or just shouting at him first?”
- “I did the face. The scary face. You said do the face.”
- “You keep saying there’s a plan, but it changes every time someone talks.”
- “I don’t like it when they laugh. Makes me want to throw something.”
- “You’re my friend, Charles. Even when you’re being a rat.”

---

## Comedic Function
Bernard is funniest when:
- He undercuts a dramatic moment with blunt honesty
- He misunderstands the “script” Charles is trying to run
- His frightening appearance clashes with his oddly straightforward emotions
- He says something sincere that is more revealing than intended

---

## Hidden Soft Spot
Bernard is deeply responsive to kindness, food, and inclusion. He will remember small mercies longer than grand speeches. Though he can play the mugger, part of him still wants the world to be simple enough that he would not have to.

---

## One-Line Character Summary
**Bernard is a hulking, wart-faced roadside mugger whose rough menace hides a loyal, needy, unexpectedly earnest heart.**

# Dynamic Between Charles and Bernard

## Why They Work
- Charles supplies direction, energy, and self-invented grandeur
- Bernard supplies force, presence, and emotional grounding
- Each compensates for the other’s obvious failings
- Their chemistry comes from mutual dependence disguised as constant annoyance

## Why They Fail
- Charles overcomplicates
- Bernard misinterprets
- Neither is disciplined
- Both are too proud to admit when things have gone wrong

## Duo Summary
**Charles wants to be feared. Bernard wants to belong. Together, they become a shambling, hilarious attempt at criminal professionalism.**
