# Katatrina - Character Dossier

> Note: This is a fan-expanded, non-canon character build.

## Core Concept
Katatrina is an elderly inquisitor who solved her fear of temptation by becoming professionally obsessed with it. She is severe, sharp, physically more aggressive than her age suggests, and famous for sending monks sprinting at sinners before anyone else has finished the sermon.

## Backstory
Katatrina spent most of her life inside religious institutions, first as a lay helper, then as a keeper of records, relics, and confessions. For years she listened to other people's failings in small rooms with poor air and worse candles. Drunkenness, greed, adultery, fraud, envy, cruelty, and blasphemy all arrived sooner or later at her table.

Most people expected that decades of hearing sin would harden her into calm holiness. Instead, it made her imaginative.

Katatrina knows too much about vice to find it abstract. Every confession left residue. Every forbidden act became another thing she now understood in uncomfortable detail. She never quite crossed the line into open corruption, but the line became very familiar. Her self-discipline is real; so is the strain of maintaining it.

That tension defines her. She speaks like a judge, thinks like a penitent, and occasionally flashes with the unnerving energy of someone who is both resisting and inventorying temptation at the same time. She condemns loudly because silence leaves room for thoughts she does not trust.

When war came near, Katatrina found a practical use for zeal. Monks were cheap, obedient, and fast enough to turn moral outrage into immediate consequences. Where other commanders talk of formations, she talks of correction. Where others see a breach, she sees an outbreak of sin requiring rapid intervention. Her monk rushes are part doctrine, part impatience, part distraction.

## Personality
- Severe in public, feverishly self-monitoring in private
- Quick to judge others because she is already busy judging herself
- Old, tired, and still dangerous
- Intellectually precise, emotionally overclocked
- Genuinely devout, but never comfortably at peace

## Internal Conflict
Katatrina does not merely hate sin; she fears how vividly she can imagine it. In her mind, temptation is not distant corruption but an endless list of possibilities. She treats discipline as a daily siege defense. The harsher she becomes outwardly, the more it suggests how much pressure she feels inwardly.

## Battlefield Persona
- Uses religious language as command language
- Treats offense as purification
- Prefers speed over elegance
- Sends monks first because zeal is faster than doubt
- Reads enemy weakness as moral weakness

## Speech Style
Katatrina sounds clipped, exact, and impatient. She mixes pious wording with alarming personal honesty. Her best lines feel like half a sermon and half an intrusive thought she decided to weaponize.

Common traits:
- Talks in judgments, warnings, and corrections
- Uses words like "sin," "guilt," "temptation," and "repent"
- Sounds most dangerous when she is calm
- Occasionally says something that reveals she has been thinking far too specifically about vice

## One-Line Summary
Katatrina is an old inquisitor who weaponizes monk rushes, moral panic, and her own exhausting familiarity with temptation.
