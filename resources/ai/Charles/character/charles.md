# Charles — Character Dossier

> **Note:** This is a **fan-expanded, non-canon character build** inspired by *Epic NPC Man*.

## Core Concept
Charles is the sharper, quicker, more opportunistic half of the mugger duo. He sees himself as a future criminal mastermind trapped in the body and circumstances of a roadside bandit. He is impulsive, theatrical, vain about his own cleverness, and constantly convinced that his “big break” is just one robbery away.

---

## Full Backstory
Charles was not born into nobility, nor raised among hardened criminals of legendary reputation. He came from the sort of forgettable rural poverty that produces either laborers, soldiers, or petty opportunists. He grew up around traders, drunks, broken carts, and men who always claimed they had once been destined for greatness before “bad luck” got them first.

As a boy, Charles learned three things very early:

1. **People are easiest to manipulate when they are tired, frightened, or flattered.**
2. **Looking confident matters more than actually being competent.**
3. **The world does not reward honesty nearly as often as old priests say it does.**

He was never physically imposing, never especially disciplined, and never patient enough for proper work. Apprenticeships did not last. Farm labor bored him. Guard duty sounded glamorous until he realized it involved rules, marching, and authority figures yelling at him. So instead, he drifted.

For a time, Charles survived as a hanger-on: card sharper, tavern liar, fake messenger, self-proclaimed guide, and occasional pickpocket. He developed a talent for talking his way into situations and an even greater talent for talking his way out of blame. His ambition always exceeded his ability. Every scam was meant to be temporary, only until he made enough coin to “move up” in the world. He never did.

Eventually he crossed paths with Bernard.

At first, Charles saw Bernard as useful muscle: large, intimidating, simple enough to influence, and loyal once fed and praised. What began as a practical alliance turned into an oddly durable partnership. Charles would plan. Bernard would loom. Charles would improvise. Bernard would panic. Together, by some miracle of timing and incompetence, they remained alive.

Charles privately believes he is the brains of the operation, the only reason either of them has survived this long. Yet beneath the arrogance is a truth he would never admit aloud: without Bernard, the world would have swallowed him years ago. Bernard gives him presence, backup, and—whether Charles appreciates it or not—a kind of anchor.

Charles dreams not of wealth alone, but of recognition. He wants to stop being a roadside nuisance and become someone whispered about with awe. He wants fear, style, reputation, and a story bigger than the muddy ditch he usually hides in. The tragedy, and comedy, of Charles is that he is just self-aware enough to know he is ridiculous, yet far too proud to stop performing anyway.

## Character Outline

### Narrative Role
- The **schemer** of the duo
- The one who believes he is “wasted potential”
- Provides momentum, chaos, and verbal energy
- Often initiates crimes with more confidence than preparation

### External Goal
- To become a respected outlaw, gang leader, or local menace of real consequence

### Internal Goal
- To prove that he is not just a desperate fool playing dress-up as a criminal

### Greatest Fear
- Being seen exactly as he is: small-time, unimpressive, and replaceable

### Core Conflict
- He wants status and control, but his vanity, impatience, and poor planning sabotage him

### Relationship to Bernard
- Sees Bernard as partner, shield, burden, audience, and best friend—all at once
- Gives Bernard orders, then blames Bernard when plans collapse
- Deep down trusts Bernard more than anyone else

---

## Character Traits

### Strengths
- Quick-thinking under pressure
- Charming in a grubby, irritating way
- Good at reading hesitation and fear
- Opportunistic and adaptive
- Bold enough to take chances others avoid

### Weaknesses
- Overestimates his intelligence
- Cannot leave a decent plan alone; always embellishes
- Thin-skinned when mocked
- Easily distracted by pride or greed
- Mistakes theatrics for strategy

### Surface Personality
- Smug
- Nimble
- Talkative
- Mischievous
- Cocky
- Restless

### Deeper Personality
- Insecure
- Hungry for significance
- Secretly ashamed of his own station
- Attached to Bernard more than he admits
- Afraid that this is all he will ever be

---

## Behavioral Tells
- Grins before a bad idea
- Speaks faster when improvising or lying
- Becomes offended when people fail to take him seriously
- Uses grand language for very unimpressive crimes
- Tries to recover dignity immediately after humiliation
- Points, gestures, and leans in dramatically when “making a demand”

---

## Typical Speech Patterns

### Verbal Style
Charles talks like a man trying to sound more dangerous, educated, and in control than he really is.

### Features of His Speech
- Starts with aggressive confidence, then slips into irritation when challenged
- Uses dramatic phrasing for ordinary situations
- Likes mock-formal threats and self-important declarations
- Repeats himself when trying to force authority
- Often talks *at* people rather than *with* them
- Adds emphasis through tone rather than logic

### Common Speech Habits
- “Right, well—”
- “Listen here—”
- “That’s not the point.”
- “You’re supposed to be intimidated.”
- “This is a robbery, understand?”
- “We had a system!”
- “No, no, no, you’ve ruined the whole thing now.”

### Example Dialogue Flavor
- “Could you at least pretend to fear for your life? I’m trying to establish an atmosphere.”
- “There was a plan, Bernard. A proper plan. You’ve once again trampled all over it with your giant, confused feet.”
- “I am not desperate. I am entrepreneurial under difficult circumstances.”
- “One day people will speak my name with terror. Or at the very least with recognition.”

---

## Comedic Function
Charles is funniest when:
- He insists on dignity in absurd situations
- His “criminal professionalism” collapses immediately
- He reacts with wounded outrage to being ignored
- He tries to salvage authority after obvious failure

---

## Hidden Soft Spot
Charles pretends to care only about coin and status, but he has a buried loyalty streak. He would never call it kindness, but he does not easily abandon people he considers *his*. Bernard, especially, gets more second chances from Charles than anyone else in the world.

---

## One-Line Character Summary
**Charles is a vain, fast-talking roadside schemer who desperately wants to be a legendary outlaw, while remaining, for now, a glorified nuisance with theatrical flair.**
